Soyuz's Fregat Upper Stage Burned Up!

Sad news from Russia. The recently launched Soyuz - which I covered a little prematurely in kNews for week 46/2017, has had an anomaly on its Fregat (frigate) upper stage!

Nominal then Boom

After a nominal launch from the new Vostochny Cosmodrome in eastern Russia, Fregat was supposed to boost its payload, a Meteor-M satellite, into its desired polar orbit. According to RussiaSpaceWeb the guidance computer believed it would launch from the older Plesetsk Cosmodrome near Moscow, Russia’s capital, or Baikonur in Kazakhstan. This led to an unplanned course correction by the flight computer followed by a wrong thrust vector. The upper stage then inevitably re-entered back into the atmosphere because it didn’t pick up enough speed to circumvent the globe in a stable orbit. The energetic event was observed by people on the other side of the globe and shows us once again:

Space is hard.
